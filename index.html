Key Findings

Data-Driven Insights

Critical discoveries from comprehensive statistical analysis

Weak Negative Correlation

Discount percentage shows a weak negative correlation with customer ratings (r = -0.155). Higher discounts are slightly associated with lower ratings, suggesting quality perception effects.

Category Differences Matter

Office Products achieves highest rating (4.31) with lowest discount (12.3%). Electronics dominates in volume (526 products) but requires higher discounts (50.8%).

Optimal Discount Zone

Products with minimal discounts (<10%) achieve the highest average ratings (4.2+). Extreme discounts (>70%) show lowest ratings, indicating potential quality concerns.

Price-Rating Relationship

Higher-priced products (Luxury >₹10K) achieve highest ratings (4.20) with lowest discounts (32.4%). Budget products require deeper discounts but maintain good ratings.

Rating Count as Predictor

Products with more reviews tend to have higher and more stable ratings. This suggests social proof and product maturity are important factors.

Model Limitations

ML models explain ~17% of rating variance (R²=0.167). Other factors like product quality, brand reputation, and delivery affect satisfaction significantly.

Prescriptive Analytics

Strategic Recommendations

Data-driven strategies to optimise Amazon's pricing approach

1

Optimise Discount Strategy

Finding: Lower discounts correlate with higher ratings

Avoid excessive discounting (>60%) which may signal quality concerns. Target 30-50% range for optimal balance.
2

Category-Specific Pricing

Finding: Categories respond differently to discounts

Office Products: Minimal discounts preserve premium perception. Electronics: Strategic discounts acceptable. Home Improvement: High discounts with high ratings - continue strategy.
3

Investigate Problem Categories

Finding: Car&Motorbike and Musical Instruments underperform

Review product quality, supplier relationships, and customer expectations for categories in "Investigate" quadrant.
4

Leverage High-Performing Segments

Finding: Premium products achieve high ratings with lower discounts

Expand Luxury (>₹10K) product offerings. Use value-based pricing rather than discount-based competition.
5

Encourage Customer Reviews

Finding: Rating count is a key predictor of satisfaction

Implement review incentive programs. Products with more reviews have more stable, higher ratings.
⚠

Risk Considerations

Caution: Key risks to monitor

Model R² is modest (~17%) - other unmeasured factors significantly impact ratings. Seasonal variations not captured. Competitor responses to pricing changes.

AI Reflection

Working with AI Tools

How AI Assisted This Project

Throughout this Business Intelligence project, AI tools (Claude) were instrumental in accelerating development while maintaining analytical rigour. The AI served as a collaborative coding partner, helping generate Python code for data cleaning, statistical analysis, and visualisation creation.

Key Learnings

  • Effective Prompting: Clear, specific prompts yield better code. Breaking complex tasks into smaller steps improved output quality.
  • Code Verification: AI-generated code requires validation against actual data - assumptions don't always match reality.
  • Iterative Refinement: Initial outputs often need refinement; treating AI as a first draft generator improved workflow.
  • Domain Knowledge: Understanding statistical concepts was essential for validating AI suggestions and interpretations.

Challenges Encountered

  • AI occasionally suggested deprecated library functions requiring manual updates
  • Statistical interpretations sometimes needed correction based on actual p-values
  • Chart styling required multiple iterations to achieve professional appearance

Skills Developed Independently

Understanding the business context, formulating the research question, interpreting statistical results in business terms, and synthesising recommendations required human judgment and domain expertise that AI assisted but could not replace.
